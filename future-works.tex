\section{Future Work}

The verified noise-flooding reduction gives a reusable formal theorem, but
the surrounding cryptographic applications are still only a first step.
In particular, there is more to verify for the CKKS approximate homomorphic
encryption scheme itself.
A major next step is to instantiate the abstract interface with CKKS and verify
the concrete scheme's IND-CPA security and approximate correctness obligations.
Another natural direction is to verify other strategies for choosing
noise-flooding parameters.
The present theorem formalizes one sufficient choice: choose the flooding
width large enough that the Pythagorean KL calculation yields the stated
IND-CPAD loss.
Existing work studies related choices through precision-loss analysis and
concrete reassessments of noise-flooding countermeasures
\cite{costache2023precision,bergamaschi2025revisiting}.
Formalizing such alternative analytic or heuristic parameter choices would
make it possible to compare security loss, output accuracy, and implementation
cost inside the same checked framework.

Finally, we can attempt to apply our program logic to the floating point computation setting as described by \cite{mw17}.
For instance, one can consider the Falcon post-quantum digital signature which was selected by NIST,
which has resisted formal verification partially due to its use of floating point numbers as opposed to finite fields.
