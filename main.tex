\begin{abstract}
Approximate homomorphic encryption schemes such as CKKS support encrypted
approximate arithmetic, but their decryption interfaces require care: small
approximation errors can become attack surfaces, and standard mitigations such
as noise flooding rely on quantitative probabilistic arguments.
We present a Rocq/SSProve formalization of the Li--Micciancio--Schultz-Wu--
Sorrell noise-flooding proof for an abstract approximate-FHE interface.
The verified theorem shows that the IND-CPAD advantage of the noise-flooded
scheme is bounded by the IND-CPA advantage of the underlying scheme plus the
expected Pythagorean loss.
The proof rests on three reusable components: a verified KL-divergence and
discrete-Gaussian analysis over countable MathComp distributions, semantic
additive-error and Pythagorean relational judgments for SSProve programs, and
a trace compiler that lifts one-call oracle replacement to the first $q$
selected adversary calls.
The main theorem is stated directly in terms of SSProve game semantics, so the
program logic is proof infrastructure rather than an additional trusted axiom.
\end{abstract}
