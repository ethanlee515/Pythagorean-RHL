\section{Checked Pythagorean Toolkit}
\label{sec:pyth-toolkit}

SSProve already supplies probabilistic programs, stateful packages, linking,
and exact relational reasoning~\cite{ssprove}.  The development adds the
quantitative layer needed by \Cref{eq:hybrid-chain}.  All rules below are Rocq
theorems over SSProve semantics.

\subsection{Completed additive error}

SSProve programs denote subdistributions over output/heap pairs.  For a
subdistribution \(\mu\) on \(X\), its completion is the distribution on
\(X\cup\{\bot\}\) defined by
\[
 \completed{\mu}(x)=\mu(x),\qquad
 \completed{\mu}(\bot)=1-\sum_x\mu(x).
\]
The additive-error judgment
\[
 \vDash\AEJ\{P\}\ c_L\approx_\delta c_R\ \{Q\}
\]
means that for every pair of initial configurations satisfying \(P\), the
completed output distributions admit a coupling in which \(Q\) fails with
probability at most \(\delta\).  For equality postconditions, a completed
statistical-distance bound yields this judgment by maximal coupling.  The
library proves consequence, sequencing, triangle, output projection, and
package-oriented variants.

Completion is important for security games with assertions.  Without it, two
programs could appear close after simply losing different amounts of mass on
failing executions.  Here failure is an observable outcome in the comparison,
even when the final cryptographic game later projects to a Boolean.

\subsection{Conditional KL transcripts}

Let \(\mathcal P,\mathcal Q\) be joint distributions on
\(\Omega^k\).  Write \(\mathcal P_i\mid a\) for the \(i\)th coordinate
conditioned on prefix \(a\in\Omega^{i-1}\).  The probability layer proves the
following countable-distribution form of Pythagorean preservation.

\begin{theorem}[Conditional-coordinate preservation]
\label{thm:pyth-probability}
Suppose \(\mathcal P,\mathcal Q\) have weight one and, for every coordinate
\(i\) and prefix \(a\), the conditional KL divergence is finite and
\[
 \KL{\mathcal P_i\mid a}{\mathcal Q_i\mid a}\leq s_i,
 \qquad s_i\geq0.
\]
Then their final-coordinate marginals satisfy
\[
 \TVD(\mathsf{marg}_k\mathcal P,
      \mathsf{marg}_k\mathcal Q)
 \leq \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k}s_i}{2}}.
\]
\end{theorem}

At pen-and-paper level, the proof expands joint mass as prefix mass times
conditional mass and groups the log likelihood ratio by coordinate.  The
resulting chain bound gives
\(\KL{\mathcal P}{\mathcal Q}\leq\sum_i s_i\).
Pinsker gives the transcript distance, and data processing under the final
projection gives the displayed conclusion.  In Rocq, the difficult part is
not the algebra but the countable analysis: absolute continuity, conditional
zero-mass prefixes, summability of positive and negative parts, and exchanging
finite coordinate sums with countable support sums are all explicit.

The semantic Pythagorean judgment
\[
 \vDash\Pyth\{P\}\ c_L\approx_s c_R\ \{Q\}
\]
asserts the existence, for each related input pair, of weight-one joint
completed transcripts whose final marginals are the programs' completed
outputs, whose conditional coordinate KL costs are bounded by the tuple
\(s\), and whose supported successful outputs satisfy \(Q\).  This is stronger
bookkeeping than an immediate statistical-distance judgment: sequencing
concatenates tuples rather than adding already square-rooted losses.

The central bridge is the checked rule
\begin{align}
\label{eq:mw-rule}
 &\vDash\Pyth\{P\}\ c_L\approx_s c_R\ \{Q\}\notag\\
 &\quad\Longrightarrow\
 \vDash\AEJ\{P\}\ c_L
   \approx_{\sqrt{\norm{s}_1/2}}c_R\
   \{o_L=o_R\}.
\end{align}
It instantiates \Cref{thm:pyth-probability} and then uses maximal coupling.

\subsection{Discrete Gaussians}

For center \(\mu\in\bbZ\) and \(\sigma>0\), the formal distribution is
\[
 D_{\bbZ,\mu,\sigma^2}(x)
 =\frac{\exp(-(x-\mu)^2/(2\sigma^2))}
        {\sum_{z\in\bbZ}\exp(-(z-\mu)^2/(2\sigma^2))}.
\]
The development proves normalization, translation invariance of the
normalizer, summability, the centered first moment, full support, and the KL
identity in \Cref{eq:dg-kl}.  The calculation expands the logarithm of the
ratio; equal variances cancel both normalizers and the quadratic terms reduce
to a linear centered moment plus \((\nu-\mu)^2\).  The moment term vanishes by
pairing \(x\) with \(-x\).  The Rocq proof separately establishes the
summability needed to justify each rearrangement.

Independent product samplers lift the scalar identity to \(\bbZ^n\):
\[
 \KL{D_{\bbZ^n,u,\sigma^2}}{D_{\bbZ^n,v,\sigma^2}}
 =\frac{\norm{u-v}_2^2}{2\sigma^2}.
\]
A verified adapter relates the MathComp tuple distribution used by the
analysis to the SSProve sampler used by the game.  Thus the one-call proof
does not assume a Gaussian axiom at the program level.

\subsection{Trace compilation}
\label{sec:trace-compiler}

The Pythagorean sequence rule handles a fixed list of fragments; an oracle
program does not expose such a list.  The trace compiler traverses concrete
SSProve syntax until a selected operation is reached.  A trace records results
of unselected calls, heap reads and writes, and samples.  Replaying those
effects against the original program recovers its residual continuation.
Malformed traces denote null code, whose behavior is covered by completion.

Two generic checked theorems are used in the security proof.

\begin{theorem}[Same-package compilation]
\label{thm:compile-same}
Subject to package typing and memory-separation conditions, exposing the first
\(q\) selected calls and resolving both selected and residual calls against
the original package preserves completed output/heap semantics.
\end{theorem}

The proof is an induction on the call bound nested with structural reasoning
about search and replay.  Each syntax constructor either extends the trace,
performs a shared state effect, or reaches the selected call.  Replay lemmas
show that valid traces recover the same residual computation, while invalid
traces have zero mass on both presentations.

\begin{theorem}[Quantitative oracle replacement]
\label{thm:compile-replace}
Let two implementations of a selected operation satisfy a one-call
Pythagorean judgment with nonnegative tuple \(s\), preserve invariant \(I\),
and obey the compiler's typing and memory restrictions.  Replacing the first
\(q\) selected calls inside a compiled program gives
\[
 \vDash\AEJ\{P_I\}\ A_{P}^{(q)}
 \approx_{\sqrt{q\norm{s}_1/2}}A_{P'}^{(q)}\ \{o_L=o_R\},
\]
where \(P_I\) synchronizes inputs and heaps satisfying \(I\).
\end{theorem}

The proof inducts over \(q\).  One selected call contributes \(s\); shared
search and replay contribute zero-budget coordinates; sequencing concatenates
the tuples.  After \(q\) calls the tuple norm is
\(q\norm{s}_1\), so applying \Cref{eq:mw-rule} once yields the stated loss.
This theorem is instantiated with \(s=(0,\epsilon_{\mathsf{nf}},0)\) in
\Cref{lem:one-call}.

\subsection{Why ordinary approximate logics are insufficient here}

An additive-error logic remains necessary for exact hops, up-to-bad arguments,
and the final triangle calculation.  Its standard sequence rule, however,
maps losses \(\delta_1,\delta_2\) to \(\delta_1+\delta_2\).  If Pinsker were
applied to each decrypt query separately, the proof would pay
\(q\sqrt{\epsilon_{\mathsf{nf}}/2}\).  The Pythagorean layer deliberately
delays that nonlinear conversion.  This is not a new security definition; it
is a verified way to retain a known KL-based cryptographic analysis when
moving from distributions to adaptive stateful programs.
